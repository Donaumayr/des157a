Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

The Chiang Kai Shek Hall in Taipei features traditional Chinese architecture with a modern twist. The building is set on a large square with a massive white marble staircase leading up to the entrance, and two bronze statues of Chiang Kai-Shek, the former President of the Republic of China. Inside, there is a massive bronze statue of Chiang Kai-Shek, as well as intricate carvings, murals, and traditional Chinese art. The hall is a popular destination a symbol of Taiwan's history and culture.

Wanhua Street

Taipei's streets are vibrant and bustling, with a wide variety of food, clothing, and goods on sale. They are a reflection of Taiwan's rich culture and history and offer a unique shopping and dining experience. Visitors can also find clothing, accessories, and souvenirs at these markets, making them an excellent place to explore and shop for gifts. Overall, Taipei's street markets are a must-visit for anyone looking to experience the vibrant energy and culture of Taiwan.

Maokong Gondola

Taipei's cable car, also known as the Maokong Gondola, takes visitors on a scenic ride up into the hills surrounding the city. The cable car offers stunning views of Taipei's skyline, mountains, and lush green forests. The ride takes around 20-30 minutes and travels from the Taipei Zoo to the village of Maokong, which is known for its tea plantations and stunning views. Visitors can explore the tea houses and sample a variety of teas while enjoying the panoramic views. The cable car is a great way to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy a peaceful, scenic experience.

Shifen Waterfall

Shifen Waterfall is a popular natural attraction near Taipei. It spans around 40 meters in height and 20 meters in width, and is surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can take a short hike to the viewing platform to admire the waterfall and take in the beautiful scenery. The area around the waterfall is also home to many shops and restaurants selling local snacks and souvenirs. It's a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy the natural beauty of Taiwan.

Shifen Old Street

Shifen Old Street is a popular tourist destination near Taipei lined with traditional buildings and shops selling local souvenirs, snacks, and handicrafts. Visitors can release lanterns into the sky, a popular tradition in Taiwan, and make wishes for good fortune. The street is known for its charming atmosphere and historic feel, with many buildings dating back to the Japanese colonial era.
